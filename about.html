François de Kock is a Taiwan-based street and documentary photographer whose work examines the quiet rhythms of everyday life in urban space.

For more than a decade, he has walked the streets of Taipei, developing a visual language grounded in close observation and compositional precision. His photography centres on the decisive moment — fleeting alignments of gesture, light, and proximity that reveal the subtle relationships between strangers in public environments.

His images are marked by an intimate, voyeuristic quality and a disciplined approach to framing, allowing the complexity of the street to resolve into coherent visual narratives.

François de Kock 是一位駐台灣的街頭與紀實攝影師，作品探索都市空間中日常生活的寧靜節奏。

十餘年來，他行走於台北街頭，發展出一套以細膩觀察與精準構圖為基礎的視覺語言。他的攝影專注於「決定性瞬間」——手勢、光線與距離在剎那間交會，揭示公共場域中陌生人之間微妙的社會關係。

他的影像以一種親密而帶有窺視感的特質著稱，搭配嚴謹的取景方式，使街頭的紛雜得以凝煉為清晰而連貫的視覺敘事。
